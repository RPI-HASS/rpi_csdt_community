Translations and Meanings of Symbols

< BACK | CONTINUE >

Each symbol has a name in Twi, the language of the Asante people. This name translates to a literal meaning and a symbolic meaning. Some of the symbols are also based on proverbs. Below some of the symbols are described with their meaning.

Symbol

Twi Name

Literal Translation

Symbolic Meaning

Proverb

Gye Nyame

"Except for God"

Supremacy of God

Only God has the power of life.

Akokonan

"The Leg of a Hen"

Mercy, Nurturing

The hen treads upon its chicks, but it does not intend to kill them.

Sankofa

"Return and Get It"

Learn from the Past

It is not taboo to return to fetch something you forgot earlier on.

Dwennimmen

"Ram's Horns"

Humility and Strength

It is the heart and not the horns that leads a ram to bully.
Adinkrahene "Cheif of the adinkra symbols" Greatness, leadership This symbol is said to have inspired the creation of the other symbols. "Hene" is the Twi word for "king" or "leader".
Pempamsie "Sew in readiness" Steadfast; prepared The links of a chain symbolize both the strength of unity and importance of preparation.

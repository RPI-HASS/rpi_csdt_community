Sports Science Team Wins Sloan Award

Last edited: March 11, 2017 by Bill Babbitt

Congratulations to Triple Helix alums John Drazan and Ben Horne for their first place win in the paper competition at the MIT Sloan Sports Analytics Conference, held this past weekend in Boston Ma. Their winning paper titled From Sports to Science: Using Basketball Analytics to Broaden the Appeal of Math and Science Among Youth, highlights the use of performance analytics in basketball to increase interest and engagement in STEM. Authored by John F. Drazan, Amy K. Loya, Benjamin D. Horne, and Ron Eglash are all from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute.

The goal of the MIT Sloan Sports Analytics Conference is to provide a forum for industry professionals (executives and leading researchers) and students to discuss the increasing role of analytics in the global sports industry. MIT Sloan is dedicated to fostering growth and innovation in this arena, and the conference enriches opportunities for learning about the sports business world. The conference is open to anyone interested in sports.

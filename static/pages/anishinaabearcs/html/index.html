Anishinaabe Arcs

CONTINUE >

Native Americans who lived in the Great Lakes region spoke a family of languages called Anishinaabe. Many of their structures were built with arcs. These include practical dwellings such as the wiigwaam and long house, and religious architecture such as the sweat lodge and mide lodge. In this website you can create simulations of traditional arc-based architecture, as well as arc structures of your own invention.
